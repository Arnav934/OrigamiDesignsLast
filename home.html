Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Camels have two rows of thick eyelashes to protect their eyes from the desert dust.

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  2. Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes.
  3. Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues.

Teddy-Bear

  1. A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
  2. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  3. Inspired by a cartoon featuring the event, shopkeeper Morris Michtom and his wife Rose made a stuffed bear with a sign 'Teddy's bear' and left it in their shop window.

Panda

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  3. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  3. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.

Flying Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. Most have red-orange eyes.
  3. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
